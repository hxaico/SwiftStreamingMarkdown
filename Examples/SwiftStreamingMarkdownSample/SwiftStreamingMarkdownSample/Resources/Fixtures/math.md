# Pythagorean theorem
Relates the sides of a right triangle and is foundational in geometry, trigonometry, and distance calculations.
$$a^2 + b^2 = c^2$$

# Euler's identity
Combines the constants e, i, pi, 1, and 0 in one compact relation often celebrated for its elegance.
$$e^(i*pi) + 1 = 0$$

# Quadratic formula
Provides the solutions to any quadratic equation where the variable appears with degree two.
$$x = (-b ± sqrt(b^2 - 4ac)) / (2a)$$

# Newton's second law
Connects net force to mass and acceleration, forming a core equation in classical mechanics.
$$F = ma$$

# Mass-energy equivalence
States that mass can be interpreted as a concentrated form of energy.
$$E = mc^2$$

# Area of a circle
Computes the two-dimensional area enclosed by a circle of radius r.
$$A = pi * r^2$$

# Circumference of a circle
Gives the perimeter length of a circle from its radius.
$$C = 2 * pi * r$$

# Volume of a sphere
Calculates the three-dimensional volume enclosed by a sphere.
$$V = (4/3) * pi * r^3$$

# Ideal gas law
Relates pressure, volume, amount of substance, and temperature for an ideal gas model.
$$PV = nRT$$

# Wave relation
Expresses wave speed as the product of frequency and wavelength.
$$v = f * lambda$$
